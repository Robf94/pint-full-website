Meet the volunteers

Cee
Founder and Trustee

Cee works alongside our volunteers daily, giving essentials and food packs to the homeless, creating relationships that have to be seen to be understood.

Toni
Trustee and Outreach Volunteer

From Hull, East Yorkshire. I found PINT on social media when I was living in London. I offered to volunteer as I had lots of spare time during the pandemic. I fell in love with the charity and enjoyed going on weekly outreach and meeting new friends. Back up North I still volunteer for PINT and we hope to set a team up here shortly.

Daiod
Trustee and Outreach Volunteer

Daiod has experienced homelessness first-hand and has previously been supported by PINT. Today, he is an avid volunteer and a well-known figure in the boxing world.

Charlotte
Trustee and Ambassador

A bubbly and vibrant individual working relentlessly on her career as a DJ and artist, who always finds time for those in need. Like Toni, Charlotte is a huge animal lover, whose concern ensures that our four legged friends on the streets gain the love they need as well as their owners.

Derek
Outreach Supervisor

Derek believes everyone deserves dignity and respect. At PINT, we provide more than just food - we are good listeners and want to engage, and give people space to talk.

Daisy
Marketing

Daisy is part of the marketing team at PINT. She is passionate about the fight to end homelessness and enjoys bringing a positive impact to the lives of the people she helps.

Oluwaseyi
Graphic/Product Designer

Design isn't just something I love; it's a tool that allows me to infuse empathy and creativity into projects that truly matter. Volunteering is my way of using my skills to contribute to initiatives that matter and touch the lives of individuals, groups, and communities that could really use a helping hand.

Ruvini
Chef/Soup Kitchen Coordinator

My experience with PINT was completely unexpected. A chance encounter during a rainy run transformed my life forever. I still recall, that I paused to catch my breath by the Streatham Odeon and ended up striking up a conversation with Cee. It happened to be an outreach day in Streatham, but that moment marked the beginning of a new chapter.

Nelhia
Project Manager

Working at PINT has been very rewarding for me as I get to serve a social responsibility with an amazing team of talented individuals, who share the same passion. From my experience joining the outreach, I am dedicated to help increase our outreach capacity, whilst making everyone aware of what we do.

Rob
Web Developer

Rob joined the charity as a junior web developer, and has built the PINT website from the ground up. He strongly believes that anyone in need should not only be able to reach out in person, but also online. Having a web presence for PINT can inform those who require our services, and to attend events and outreaches that take place throughout each week.
